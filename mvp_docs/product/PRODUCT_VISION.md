# PRODUCT_VISION.md

# Product Name
AI-Powered IELTS CBT Simulator

---

# Vision Statement

Build a realistic and trustworthy IELTS preparation platform that combines strict examiner-style evaluation, transparent scoring, and AI-powered feedback to help learners prepare effectively for real IELTS examinations.

The platform aims to simulate authentic IELTS test conditions while providing explainable and actionable feedback that traditional practice platforms often fail to deliver.

---

# Core Product Positioning

The product is positioned as:

> "An AI-powered IELTS CBT simulator with strict examiner-style evaluation and explainable scoring."

The platform prioritizes:
- realism over motivational scoring,
- assessment accuracy over entertainment,
- and transparent evaluation over generic AI feedback.

---

# Primary Goals

## 1. Simulate Real IELTS Experience

Provide a test-taking experience that closely resembles official IELTS CBT environments, including:
- strict timing,
- structured test flow,
- realistic section behavior,
- and authentic evaluation logic.

---

## 2. Deliver Trustworthy Evaluation

Provide scoring and feedback that feel:
- consistent,
- evidence-based,
- transparent,
- and aligned with IELTS band descriptors.

The platform should avoid inflated scoring and overly supportive AI behavior.

---

## 3. Enable Explainable Learning

Users should not only receive scores, but also understand:
- why they received those scores,
- which weaknesses affected performance,
- and how to improve strategically.

---

## 4. Build Scalable AI Assessment Infrastructure

The MVP should establish a strong architectural foundation for future expansion, including:
- additional IELTS sections,
- adaptive learning systems,
- personalized recommendations,
- analytics,
- and advanced speaking interactions.

---

# Target Users

## Primary Audience

IELTS candidates who:
- are preparing independently,
- need realistic evaluation,
- want actionable feedback,
- and seek affordable alternatives to human tutors.

---

## User Characteristics

Typical users may:
- target Band 6.0–8.0,
- prepare for academic or migration purposes,
- struggle with Writing and Speaking improvement,
- and lack access to high-quality personalized feedback.

---

# MVP Focus

The MVP focuses on:
- Writing Task 2
- Speaking Part 2

These modules are prioritized because they:
- represent the highest learning pain points,
- require active feedback,
- and benefit most from AI-assisted evaluation.

---

# Product Philosophy

The platform follows several core principles:

## Strict Examiner Philosophy

The evaluation system should behave conservatively and realistically.

If uncertainty exists between two band levels, the lower band should generally be preferred unless strong evidence supports the higher score.

---

## Transparency First

Users should see:
- score breakdowns,
- evidence-based explanations,
- and specific weaknesses affecting performance.

The platform should avoid black-box scoring behavior.

---

## Hybrid Evaluation System

The platform should combine:
- deterministic linguistic metrics,
- rule-based scoring logic,
- and LLM-assisted qualitative analysis.

AI should assist evaluation, not fully control it.

---

## Scalable by Design

Even though the MVP targets single-user workflows, the architecture should remain modular and scalable for future growth.

---

# Differentiation

The platform differentiates itself from generic AI IELTS tools by emphasizing:

- realistic examiner-style scoring,
- transparent assessment logic,
- evidence-backed feedback,
- structured evaluation pipelines,
- and hybrid rule + AI scoring systems.

---

# Long-Term Direction

Potential future expansion may include:
- Reading and Listening modules,
- adaptive learning systems,
- progress tracking,
- AI conversational speaking interviews,
- teacher dashboards,
- and personalized improvement roadmaps.

---

# Technical Direction

The MVP is planned using:
- Next.js + TypeScript
- Firebase ecosystem
- Gemini API
- PWA architecture
- Spec-Driven Development
- AI-assisted coding workflows

---

# Non-Goals for MVP

The MVP intentionally excludes:
- gamification-heavy systems,
- leaderboard mechanics,
- social learning features,
- full realtime speaking conversations,
- and advanced subscription systems.

The initial focus is evaluation quality and assessment realism.

---

# Success Criteria

The MVP will be considered successful if users perceive the platform as:

- realistic,
- trustworthy,
- academically credible,
- and genuinely useful for IELTS preparation.

Evaluation quality and user trust are prioritized over feature quantity.
